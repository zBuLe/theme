## Overview

Vertebrates are animals that possess a vertebral column (backbone) and an internal skeleton made of bone or cartilage. They belong to the subphylum **Vertebrata**, within the phylum **Chordata**. Vertebrates are among the most structurally complex organisms on Earth and include both aquatic and terrestrial species.

---

## Key Characteristics

- Backbone or spinal column
- Bilateral symmetry
- Closed circulatory system with a heart
- Complex nervous system with a well-developed brain
- Endoskeleton (bone or cartilage)
- Advanced sensory organs

---

## Major Groups of Vertebrates

### 1. Fish

- Live in water
- Gills for respiration
- Fins for movement
- Usually covered in scales
- Examples: Salmon, sharks, goldfish

### 2. Amphibians

- Begin life in water, often move to land as adults
- Moist skin without scales
- Undergo metamorphosis
- Examples: Frogs, salamanders, newts

### 3. Reptiles

- Dry, scaly skin
- Lay shelled eggs on land
- Cold-blooded (ectothermic)
- Examples: Snakes, lizards, turtles

### 4. Birds

- Feathers and wings
- Warm-blooded (endothermic)
- Lay hard-shelled eggs
- Lightweight skeleton
- Examples: Eagles, sparrows, penguins

### 5. Mammals

- Hair or fur
- Mammary glands for feeding young
- Warm-blooded
- Highly developed brains
- Examples: Humans, whales, elephants

---

## Evolutionary Significance

Vertebrates evolved over 500 million years ago from early chordates. Their internal skeleton allowed larger body sizes and more efficient movement, leading to extensive diversification and ecological dominance in many environments.

---

## Ecological Importance

Vertebrates play essential roles in ecosystems:

- Predators regulate prey populations
- Herbivores influence plant distribution
- Scavengers recycle nutrients
- Many act as pollinators or seed dispersers

## Summary

Vertebrates represent a highly successful lineage of animals distinguished by structural complexity, adaptive diversity, and wide ecological influence. Their evolutionary innovations—especially the backbone and advanced nervous system—enabled them to occupy nearly every habitat on Earth.
